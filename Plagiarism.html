What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else’s work or ideas without proper acknowledgment...

Examples of Plagiarism:

  • Copying text from a source without citation.
  • Rephrasing ideas without giving credit.
  • Submitting others’ work as your own.

Why Plagiarism Matters?

Plagiarism undermines academic integrity and devalues the hard work of others...

Types of Plagiarism

  • Direct Plagiarism: Copying another’s work word-for-word without credit.
  • Self-Plagiarism: Reusing your own work for multiple purposes without acknowledgment.
  • Mosaic Plagiarism: Patching together phrases from multiple sources.
  • Accidental Plagiarism: Failing to cite sources correctly.

Plagiarism Detection Tools

  • Turnitin: A widely used tool in academic institutions.
  • Grammarly: Checks for plagiarism alongside grammar corrections.
  • Quetext: Offers advanced detection for text similarities.

How to Avoid Plagiarism?

Cite sources, use tools like Turnitin, and learn proper referencing methods.

Consequences of Plagiarism?

It can lead to academic penalties, legal issues, and reputational harm.
